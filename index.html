About Us

We've been together for over ten years! …I know, can you believe it?


Our journey starts all the way back in "Toon-town," where Kersti somehow fell for Alec during his bachelor-pad days — he was living his best life, though his sister Jenna had to endure it all and was decidedly not living hers. After an incredible seven months together, Kersti took a leap of faith and transferred to West Virginia University so they could close the distance. One of their very first pictures together? Yes — on a burning couch. Go Eers!


They tried to live as gloriously as possible on a college budget, interning in different cities as they figured out where to start their post-college life — all while saving every penny for travel. Fast forward to graduation, which wasn't exactly what you'd expect. Neither of them walked across a stage or shook a hand. Instead, they ate cake in Kersti's backyard and watched a half-hearted PowerPoint slide with their names on it. Thanks, COVID!


Graduation led them to engineering jobs in Philadelphia, PA, where they were truly on their own for the first time. No more meal swipes, weekly allowances, or PRT cars shuttling them around — they were real adults. Philly showed them how much they loved eating out and discovering new coffee shops. It was their first big-city experience, and to this day it holds some of their most cherished memories.


As life progressed, Kersti's job brought them down to Raleigh, NC, where they tried to find downtown and didn't realize they'd already driven through it. No more skyline filled with skyscrapers and bustling streets. But they met some of the most incredible people of their lives while figuring out their own path. As the friend group grew, so did the roots — and it became clear this was where Kersti and Alec would plant theirs.


In the spring of 2024, they got engaged in Sedona, AZ, followed by a house purchase that fall — an exciting year, to say the least. Since then, they've dove into home design and weekend projects while keeping their love for city dining and coffee shop explorations very much alive.


After a lonnnnng three-year engagement, they finally decided, "I guess it's time to plan our wedding" — which is exactly what brought you here today.


Thank you for being such an instrumental part of their lives. Without you, none of this would exist — and for that, they are endlessly grateful.
